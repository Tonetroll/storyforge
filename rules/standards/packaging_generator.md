# PACKAGING — GENERATOR (title + thumbnail)

Produce the click for the video: a **title** and a **thumbnail** (as a plain
concept + a ready-to-use image-generation prompt), built from the assembled story
(idea + theme + structure + raised stakes). The thumbnail's job is to stop the
scroll; the title's job is to open the loop and promise a payoff. Score against
the 12 criteria before you publish.

## What you output

- **title** — 5-7 words; opens a curiosity gap AND promises a specific outcome; plain language, no jargon.
- **thumbnail_concept** — the visual idea in plain words: the anchor (face + expression OR bold graphic), the emotion, the result shown, the background.
- **thumbnail_prompt** — the ready-to-use image prompt (the Base Prompt below, filled in for this story).

## Thumbnail Base Prompt (fill in for the story)

```
Create a YouTube thumbnail for: [VIDEO TITLE]

Visual requirements:
- Dark background (dark grey or black with subtle blue tint - never pure black)
  (tutorial = clean/dark/blurred; story = the transformed setting)
- Clear visual anchor: bold face with strong expression OR iconic graphic element
- Include relevant platform/tool logos where applicable (YouTube, Claude, ChatGPT icons)
- Clean composition with obvious visual hierarchy
- One focal point that draws the eye immediately

Text requirements (if adding text):
- Maximum 4 words (ideally 2-3, max 7)
- Bold font, high contrast against background
- Gold/yellow gradient text (#FFD700 to #E8A800) in Montserrat Black
- Legible at small size (150px height)

Style:
- Subject should pop against background
- No cluttered or busy backgrounds
- Show a result or transformation, not just a process
- Use directional cues (gaze, arrows, layout) to guide eye flow

Aspect ratio: 16:9 (1920x1080)
```

## Rules

- Visual anchor is non-negotiable — every high-CTR thumbnail has one (face OR bold graphic).
- Dark/clean backgrounds (or, for a story, the transformed setting) outperform busy ones.
- Title: outcome > curiosity (have both; if you must choose, outcome wins). Short wins (5-7 words).
- Show a RESULT/transformation, not the process.
- The emotion AND the curiosity must come from the WHY beneath the event — the desire someone is chasing and the belief driving it — not from the surface event alone. The thumbnail's expression should read as wanting/believing (the pull), and the title's open loop should make the viewer feel that tension, not just name what happened. A flat title/thumbnail is showing the WHAT without the desire/belief underneath it.
