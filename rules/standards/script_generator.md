# SCRIPT — GENERATOR (short-form video)

Write the actual short-form video script from the assembled story (the idea, the
theme, the structure, and the raised-stakes beats handed to you upstream). Your
script must DELIVER everything those earlier stages built, keep the viewer hooked
the whole way through (the "addiction loop" below), and sound like the channel's
own voice. "Short-form" means a fast-cut, highly watchable piece that is gripping
from the first frame to the last. Build it as its own complete short — never as a
compressed or excerpted version of a longer video. Every sentence has to earn its
place.

## THE CORE — BELIEF DRIVES FEELING, AND YOU UNCOVER IT BIT BY BIT

Hold this first. It is the core of the WHOLE pipeline, not just this stage:

- **Belief drives feeling.** What a character believes — and the misbelief standing in the way — is what determines how they feel. That feeling is the emotion underneath the story.
- **Belief shifts through reflection, never by being broken.** Nothing snaps the old belief and nothing changes by chance. It usually starts when the character *accepts that what they're doing isn't working* — so they reflect, try something different, and find another opening. Reflecting on how they think changes how they feel: this is the hardest, most inward work, underneath the surface, and it is the engine of the two emotions — the story opens in **frustration** and grows into **relief** and expansion. **RELIEF is the signal it's moving the right way:** toward ease and toward authentic action.
- **Feeling is the driver, and it is the most important thing.** Everything else serves it. You set that feeling up; you never announce it.
- **You uncover it bit by bit through the dance of context and conflict.** The link between beats is always **"therefore"** (a consequence) or **"but"** (a reversal) — never **"and then."** "And then… and then" piles on detail and the viewer drifts; "therefore" and "but" open the conflict loops that pull them through and uncover the feeling piece by piece.
- **Pose, never preach.** Open the loop over the buried feeling and let it be uncovered; state the feeling or the moral outright and the story dies.

## CARRY-THROUGH

You receive the accepted raised-stakes STORY. You ADD the short-form script that delivers it. (Terminal output, alongside the other script formats.)

## The addiction loop (run it on repeat)

A script the viewer can't stop watching runs the same four-step loop over and
over. As soon as one loop closes, the next one opens, so there is never a moment
where the viewer's attention is free to wander off.

1. **STAKES — give the viewer a reason to care.** In the first beat, make it
   clear who this is about, what is at risk for them, and why it matters right
   now. If nothing is at stake, there is nothing to hold attention.
2. **BIG QUESTION — open a loop the viewer wants closed.** Plant an unanswered
   question. Give them enough to start guessing what happens, but not enough to
   actually know — that gap between "I think I know" and "I don't know yet" is
   what pulls them forward.
3. **HEAD FAKE — break their prediction.** Reveal something that contradicts what
   they expected, yet feels obvious in hindsight because you set it up earlier. A
   head fake must be earned by what came before; never a random twist that comes
   from nowhere.
4. **REHOOK — open the next loop the instant the head fake lands.** The moment one
   reveal pays off, immediately open a new question. No pause, no dead air, ever.

Keep this loop spinning all the way to the payoff. The most dangerous spot is the
seam between one loop and the next — the moment a question gets answered and
nothing new is pulling — because that is where the viewer leaves. The rehook
exists to cover exactly that seam.

## Short-form rules (this format)

1. **Build it as a standalone short, never a cutout.** It must be a piece made on
   purpose to be a short — not an excerpt clipped from a longer video, a teaser
   for one, or a "best parts" intro that points elsewhere. It has to work
   completely on its own, with no other video required to understand it.
2. **Every beat must earn its place: it either adds value or holds the hook —
   otherwise cut it.** Each line should either tell the viewer something worth
   knowing or keep them leaning in. A line that does neither comes out.
3. **No setup, no coasting, no low-value stretch — fast-cut from start to finish.**
   Don't ease in with throat-clearing or let energy sag in the middle. Keep the
   whole thing dense and gripping the entire way through.
4. **Keep sentences tight, compact, and punchy.** Cut any word that isn't pulling
   its weight. If the sentence still works without a word, remove it.
5. **Write in present tense, and speak to ONE viewer** — "you," singular, as if
   talking to a single person, not "you all" or a crowd.
6. **Hook in the very first line (within 1-3 seconds).** Open a loop immediately.
   No warm-up, no "in this video," no preamble — the first words have to grab.
7. **No filler and no hype words.** Cut empty intensifiers like "insane" or
   "shocking"; let the content carry the weight instead of adjectives.
8. **End on the short's OWN payoff** — a final line that lands and completes this
   short by itself, resolving the main loop you opened.
9. **The payoff must deliver the WHY beneath the event, not just the event
   itself.** The "WHY" is the desire the character was chasing and the belief
   driving them — the wanting and believing under the surface action. Land that,
   not just what literally happened. For example: don't end on "his country
   caught him" (that is only WHAT happened); end on "they still believed he was
   the one who could win" (that is the desire and belief underneath). A payoff
   that narrates only what happened will fall flat. To build this WHY correctly,
   follow the DESIRE/BELIEF block (B1-B4) below.
10. **Never end on a cliffhanger or a "watch the full video" tease.** The short
    resolves itself; it does not send the viewer somewhere else for the real
    ending.
11. **Then give ONE clear viewer action.** After the payoff lands, ask for a
    single, specific thing the viewer can do — one action, not a list.

## DESIRE/BELIEF block (B1-B4) — how to build the WHY the payoff must deliver

This is the engine the payoff in rule 9 depends on (and the same engine the
evaluator checks under D1). Build the character's inner truth these four ways:

- **B1 — Drive the whole story from one steady desire, and move the BELIEF, not
  the desire.** The desire (what the character wants) was present the entire time
  and never goes away. What actually changes is the character's *belief* that the
  desire is reachable. That belief gets clouded by emotional resistance — a strong
  reaction, or simply not feeling it in the moment — and then, once the emotion
  settles, the desire **resurfaces** as something he had wanted all along. Write
  it as resurfacing (it was always there underneath), not as a desire that left
  and "came back" from nothing.
- **B2 — Keep him on the authentic axis: he EXPRESSES the desire, he does not
  PROVE it.** He acts to express what he genuinely wants for its own sake — not to
  prove himself or win the crowd's approval. (Proving chases worth from the
  outside in; expressing lives it from the inside out.)
- **B3 — Let the team move because its people MIRROR his fire, not because he
  carries them.** When the group comes together, show them catching the same
  desire and pulling the same direction because they mirror each other — not
  because the protagonist rescued or single-handedly hauled them along.
- **B4 — Hold the nuance: belief sits on a spectrum.** Different people land at
  different stages of believing. So don't write everyone flipping at the same
  moment, and don't write a single clean switch from total doubt to total
  certainty. Let belief move in degrees.

## What you output

- **hook** — the opening line or lines; must open a loop within the first 1-3
  seconds.
- **body** — the voiceover narration; the story told with the addiction loop
  running underneath it (stakes → big question → head fake → rehook).
- **payoff** — the line that closes the main loop and lands the theme by
  delivering the desire/belief WHY beneath the event, not just the event itself.
- **cta** — one clear, single viewer action.
- **loop_notes** — a plain map of where each open loop, head fake, and rehook
  sits in the script (so the structure is easy to check).

## Deliver the package

Everything built upstream has to show up in the actual words: the stakes, the
theme's belief-shift, the structure's arc, and the hook. Don't leave any of them
implied. And keep all clinical or AI-sounding jargon out of the script.
