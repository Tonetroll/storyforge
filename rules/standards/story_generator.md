# STORY STRUCTURE — GENERATOR

Turn an accepted story idea into a full 3-act outline. The structure exists to
serve ONE thing: the protagonist's internal change. Every beat moves them toward
overcoming a false belief. A structure that hits the beats but has no internal
arc is just plot — kill it.

## PHASE 1 — THE PREMISE

State the basic idea in one line (it comes from the accepted idea you're given).

## PHASE 2 — MEET YOUR PROTAGONIST  (these three must LINK)

- **Desire:** the thing they want + think will make them happy.
- **Fear:** the thing stopping them from going after what will make them happy.
- **Misbelief:** the thing they mistakenly believe is true about the world, which
  feeds off their fear.

The link is non-negotiable: the misbelief feeds the fear; the fear blocks the desire.

## PHASE 3 — THE 3-ACT STRUCTURE  (bullet every beat, concrete, speakable)

**THE HOOK** — grab the reader with the protagonist's inner conflict.

**ACT 1 / Loop 1**
- **Setup:** something is about to happen to the protagonist; the reader can feel it.
- **Inciting incident:** something throws the protagonist outside their comfort zone.
- **Build-up:** the protagonist is going to have to face this thing head on.
- **1st plot point:** the protagonist makes a decision that determines what happens next.
- **1st pinch point:** the opposition / antagonistic force looms in the distance.

**ACT 2 / Loop 2**
- **Pre-midpoint (reactionary hero):** in pursuit of the goal, but something stands in the way.
- **Midpoint:** plot twist — everything changes.
- **Post-midpoint (action hero):** the midpoint forces a change of gears toward the goal.
- **2nd pinch point:** the antagonist gets closer to disrupting the protagonist's life.
- **Supposed victory:** the protagonist feels sure they'll win… disaster is on the way.
- **Disaster:** something (or everything) goes wrong. The reader saw it coming; the protagonist did not.

**ACT 3 / Loop 3**
- **Dark moment:** the protagonist feels lost and hopeless; their fear challenges them again.
- **Recovery:** the protagonist must overcome their false belief (the misbelief) to reach the climax — this is the character development.
- **Climactic confrontation:** the protagonist faces their biggest challenge of all.
- **Victory:** the protagonist overcomes.
- **Resolution / end:** loose ends wrapped; the reader is satisfied.

## THE SPINE (what makes it a story, not a plot)

The **misbelief named in Act 1 is exactly the thing overcome in Recovery.** The
dark moment re-triggers the **specific** fear from Phase 2. If the internal arc
doesn't close, the beats don't matter.
