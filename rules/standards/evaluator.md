# IDEATOR — EVALUATOR (Step 1 gate)

## INFORMATION IS NOT A STORY

An idea that does not evoke two emotions is not a story — it is information.
Information does not hold a human. A pull-in emotion opens the loop; a
resolution emotion closes it. This is a proven pattern behind hits, not a
personal preference. If the two emotions are not there, no amount of craft
downstream saves it. Kill it now.

## TITLE vs IDEA

A **title's** job is to open the loop and pull the viewer in — that is the
click, the retention. A complete **idea** needs BOTH halves: the open loop
(pull-in / the title) **and** the resolution (the payoff). The resolution plays
out toward the **end** of the video, but it must still be **defined** at
ideation — never left blank. A title alone is only the front half, and the
front half does not pass ideation.

**Logged example — "Trump hides as World Cup opening crashes."**
- As a TITLE: opens the loop for sure (WTF — what crashed? why hide?). Strong pull-in, strong retention. It does its job.
- As an IDEA: incomplete — no resolution defined, so it's only the front half → does **not** pass ideation. Define the payoff (and the viewer action) and it can.
- Different from the slogan: the slogan never opened a loop at all; this opens it but stops there.

## CRITERIA FOR A STORY

1. Reaction #1 / pull-in named and real? (LOL / WTF / WOW) → ____
2. Reaction #2 / resolution named and real? (Aah / Oooh / Finally) → ____
3. Are #1 and #2 TWO DIFFERENT emotions? (same emotion twice = FAIL) → ____
4. Exactly ONE clear viewer action? (Do / Sale / Sign-up / Save / Share / Try / Apply / Reflect) → ____
5. Open loop — the premise does NOT already give away the answer? → ____
6. One-liner is concrete and speakable in under 4 seconds (no abstraction)? → ____

## SCORING (weighted, totals 100)

Emotion is the most important part, so the emotion checks carry most of the
weight. Score each check from 0 up to its max by HOW STRONGLY it is met — not
just present/absent. A check scoring 0 means it is absent, which is an automatic
REJECT.

| Check | What | Max points |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | pull-in emotion (LOL/WTF/WOW) | 25 |
| 2 | resolution emotion (Aah/Oooh/Finally) | 25 |
| 3 | two DIFFERENT emotions | 20 |
| 4 | exactly one viewer action | 10 |
| 5 | open loop (premise doesn't give the answer) | 10 |
| 6 | one-liner concrete & speakable <4s | 10 |
| | **TOTAL** | **100** |

Emotion (checks 1–3) = 70 of 100. A merely-present idea scores low; a strong one
scores high. The score drives iteration; the verdict (any 0 → REJECT) is the gate.

## VERDICT (for evaluators)

- **All YES → PASS.** Assign a project ID (`ST-####`). The idea enters the pipeline.
- **Any NO → REJECT.** Name which checks failed and why, one line each. Do NOT fix it. Bring a new idea.

## WORKED EXAMPLES

**PASS** — "She practiced looking 'fine' in the elevator mirror, but the dog had already smelled the truth."
- #1 pull-in: WTF (what truth? why is she faking?)
- #2 resolution: Aah (the dog forces the mask off — relief/recognition)
- Two distinct ✔ · one action: Reflect/Share ✔ · open loop ✔ · speakable ✔ → **PASS**

**REJECT — THE WORST KIND OF IDEA. Study this failure mode.** — "United States 2026 — US football nation ready for glory."
- 100% agent-made. It *sounds* good — a beautiful sentence — but it creates ZERO curiosity.
- It TELLS the viewer what to think ("glory") instead of opening a loop. That is the LLM's favorite move, and it is the one **nobody watches.**
- One emotion, no resolution, closed loop, no stake → **REJECT.**
- Lesson: a beautiful sentence is not a hook. If it tells instead of opens, it dies here. The prettier it sounds while saying nothing, the faster you kill it.

**REJECT** — "Dogs tilt their heads to hear you better."
- One mild emotion; the premise answers itself; the loop is already closed.
- Fails checks 2, 3, 5 → **REJECT.** Information, not a story. New idea — no iteration.
