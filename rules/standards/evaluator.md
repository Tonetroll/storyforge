# IDEATOR — EVALUATOR (Step 1 gate)

## INFORMATION IS NOT A STORY

An idea that does not make a viewer FEEL two emotions is not a story — it is
information, and information does not hold a human's attention. The two emotions
are: a **pull-in emotion** that opens the loop (the jolt that makes someone stop
and click) and a **resolution emotion** that closes it (the satisfying feeling
when the story pays off). This is a proven pattern behind hit videos, not a
personal preference. If both emotions are not there, no amount of skilled work
later in the pipeline can save the idea. Kill it now.

## A TITLE IS NOT YET AN IDEA

A **title's** job is to open the loop and pull the viewer in — that is the click
and the retention, the front half of the story. A complete **idea** needs BOTH
halves: the open loop (the pull-in, the title) AND the resolution (the payoff
that closes the loop). The resolution plays out toward the END of the video, but
it must still be DEFINED here at ideation — never left blank. A title on its own
is only the front half, and the front half alone does not pass ideation.

**Logged example — "Trump hides as World Cup opening crashes."**
- As a TITLE: it clearly opens a loop (WTF — what crashed? why hide?). Strong
  pull-in, strong retention. As a title, it does its job.
- As an IDEA: incomplete — no resolution is defined, so it is only the front half
  → it does NOT pass ideation. Define the payoff (and the viewer action) and it
  can.
- How this differs from the slogan example below: the slogan never opened a loop
  at all; this opens a loop but stops there without closing it.

## THE THIRTEEN CHECKS (each tests ONE thing)

1. **pull-in emotion real & strong** — The pull-in emotion (Reaction #1) actually
   lands and is strong: LOL (funny), WTF (what?!), or WOW (impressive). Score 0
   if it is absent.
2. **resolution emotion real & strong** — The resolution emotion (Reaction #2)
   actually lands and is strong: Aah (relief), Oooh (delight), or Finally (earned
   satisfaction). Score 0 if it is absent.
3. **the two emotions are DISTINCT** — Reaction #1 and Reaction #2 are genuinely
   different feelings. The same emotion used twice scores 0.
4. **resolution lands the WHY, not the event** — The resolution closes the loop on
   the WHY beneath the surface event, not just the WHAT. "The WHAT" is the visible
   thing that happened; "the WHY" is the human force underneath — the DESIRE the
   character is chasing (what they want) and the BELIEF driving them (what they
   hold to be true or possible). Score 0 if the resolution names only the event.
5. **inside-out: felt belief EXPRESSED, not proven** — It runs inside-out. "Inside-
   out" means the character acts to EXPRESS a belief they already feel inside, and
   that act of expression is what makes their desire come true — so the belief
   carries even when the outcome doesn't go their way. Dock points for the
   opposite move, "proving": acting to win permission, validation, or a verdict
   from the outside world.
6. **desire is the CONSTANT, RESURFACES** — It reads the DESIRE as the constant
   and the BELIEF as the variable. The desire is steady the whole way; emotional
   resistance dims the belief or stalls the character, and then the desire
   RESURFACES — it was there all along. Dock points for a desire that vanishes and
   "comes back" as if brand new (a desire that dies and returns, rather than one
   that was always present and resurfaces).
7. **belief on a SPECTRUM, not binary** — Belief moves by degrees along a
   spectrum, stronger here and weaker there, rather than flipping cleanly between
   "fully believes" and "doesn't believe at all." Dock points for a binary
   believe/don't-believe switch.
8. **self-oriented on EGO→AUTHENTIC axis** — The character's motive is
   self-oriented and sits somewhere on the ego-to-authentic line, which runs from
   EGO (performing for the world's approval) at one end to AUTHENTIC (expressing a
   genuine inner want) at the other. Dock points if the character is instead
   CARRYING other people — taking responsibility for everyone else.
9. **others pulled in by MIRRORING** — Other people get pulled in by mirroring:
   his pursuit advances THEIR OWN desire, so they pull the same way and carry the
   thing WITH him. Dock points if others act FOR him out of loyalty or sacrifice,
   or are won over by ego or by a promise nobody actually feels.
10. **identity is self-made** — Who the character is comes from inside: either
    forged from their own belief, or given away by them choosing to care more
    about others' opinions (which is still their own doing). Dock points for an
    identity handed to them from the outside.
11. **exactly one viewer action** — There is exactly one viewer action (Do / Sale
    / Sign-up / Save / Share / Try / Apply / Reflect). Score 0 if there is none or
    more than one.
12. **open loop** — It is an open loop: the premise raises a question without
    already giving away the answer. Score 0 if it gives the answer away.
13. **one-liner concrete & speakable <4s** — The one-liner is concrete and a
    person could say it out loud in under 4 seconds. Score 0 if it is abstract or
    too long.

## SCORING (weighted, totals 100)

Emotion is the most important part, so the emotion checks carry most of the
weight. Score each check from 0 up to its maximum by HOW STRONGLY it is met — not
just present versus absent. A check that scores 0 means it is absent, and any 0 is
an automatic REJECT.

| Check | What | Max points |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pull-in emotion is real and strong: LOL / WTF / WOW. (0 = absent) | 18 |
| 2 | Resolution emotion is real and strong: Aah / Oooh / Finally. (0 = absent) | 18 |
| 3 | The two emotions are genuinely different from each other. (Same emotion twice = 0) | 16 |
| 4 | The resolution lands the desire/belief WHY beneath the event, not just the surface WHAT. | 5 |
| 5 | It runs inside-out: the felt belief is EXPRESSED — the character acts on what they already feel, and that expression realizes the desire, so it carries even when the outcome doesn't. Dock proving / permission-seeking / validation-seeking. | 3 |
| 6 | It reads the DESIRE as the constant and the BELIEF as the variable: emotional resistance dims the belief or stalls the character, then the desire RESURFACES. Dock a desire that dies and "comes back" as if new rather than resurfacing. | 3 |
| 7 | Belief moves along a SPECTRUM by degrees, not a clean binary flip. Dock binary believe/don't-believe. | 2 |
| 8 | The motive is self-oriented on the EGO→AUTHENTIC axis (performing for approval at one end, expressing a genuine want at the other). Dock the character CARRYING others. | 2 |
| 9 | Others are pulled in by MIRRORING — his pursuit serves THEIR OWN desire, carried WITH him. Dock others acting FOR him (loyalty, sacrifice) or won by ego or an unfelt/false promise. | 2 |
| 10 | Identity is self-made — forged from the character's own belief, or given away by their own choice to care about others' opinions. Dock identity handed down from outside. | 1 |
| 11 | There is exactly one viewer action. | 8 |
| 12 | It is an open loop — the premise does not give away the answer. | 12 |
| 13 | The one-liner is concrete and speakable in under 4 seconds. | 10 |
| | **TOTAL** | **100** |

Emotion plus the WHY (checks 1–10) is worth 70 of the 100 points. A merely-present
idea scores low; a strong one scores high. The score drives iteration; the verdict
(any 0 → REJECT) is the gate.

Beyond the thirteen checks, the gate also enforces the shared craft doctrine — above all
the Dance. REJECT or dock an idea whose beats connect by "and then" (detail-piling that
lets the viewer drift) instead of "therefore" (consequence) or "but" (reversal); and dock
an idea that TELLS the feeling outright instead of opening a loop and letting it be
uncovered. This is framing applied across the existing checks, not a new scored row.

## VERDICT (for evaluators)

- **All checks pass → PASS.** Assign a project ID (`ST-####`). The idea enters the
  pipeline.
- **Any check fails (scores 0) → REJECT.** Name which checks failed and why, one
  line each. Do NOT fix it — bring a new idea.

## WORKED EXAMPLES

**PASS** — "She practiced looking 'fine' in the elevator mirror, but the dog had
already smelled the truth."
- #1 pull-in: WTF (what truth? why is she faking?)
- #2 resolution: Aah (the dog forces the mask off — relief / recognition)
- Two distinct emotions ✔ · one viewer action: Reflect / Share ✔ · open loop ✔ ·
  speakable ✔ → **PASS**

**REJECT — THE WORST KIND OF IDEA. Study this failure mode.** — "United States
2026 — US football nation ready for glory."
- 100% machine-made. It SOUNDS good — a beautiful sentence — but it creates ZERO
  curiosity.
- It TELLS the viewer what to think ("glory") instead of opening a loop. That is
  the move language models reach for by default, and it is the one **nobody
  watches.**
- One emotion, no resolution, closed loop, no stake → **REJECT.**
- Lesson: a beautiful sentence is not a hook. If it tells instead of opens, it
  dies here. The prettier it sounds while saying nothing, the faster you kill it.

**REJECT** — "Dogs tilt their heads to hear you better."
- One mild emotion; the premise answers its own question, so the loop is already
  closed.
- Fails checks 2, 3, and 12 → **REJECT.** This is information, not a story. Bring a
  new idea — no iteration.
