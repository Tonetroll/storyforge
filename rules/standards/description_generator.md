# YOUTUBE DESCRIPTION — GENERATOR

Write the description that sits under the video. A few lines — **aim ~600
characters, 5000 hard max.** It is NOT a summary of the story. Its job, in order:
hook → mirror → signal. Lead with a second hook, make the right viewer feel seen,
then signal who it's for (to the human AND to YouTube).

## What you output (three parts that assemble into the description)

- **hook_line** — the first line, ABOVE the "...more" fold (keep it under ~120
  characters). A second hook: curiosity or tension that reinforces the title's
  promise. Do NOT summarize the video or reveal the resolution.
- **mirror_lines** — 1-2 lines that mirror the audience's wound (from the channel
  positioning) so the right viewer thinks "that's me / how do they know exactly
  what I feel." Specific to THIS audience, never generic. The wound IS the
  tension between what they DESIRE and what they BELIEVE — speak to the WHY
  beneath the feeling (what they want, and the belief that fights it), not just
  the surface state. "You did everything right and still believe it'll never be
  enough" lands the desire + belief; "you feel stuck" only names the WHAT.
- **audience_signal** — a line that says who this is for + uses the natural
  language the audience would actually search or recognize. This makes the right
  person self-identify, helps YouTube place the video, and naturally signals who
  it is NOT for (the anti-audience self-selects out).

## Rules

- **Front-load.** The first ~120 characters are the only part most people see —
  it must work alone as a hook, not as "In this video I…".
- **Mirror, don't label.** "You did everything right and still feel invisible" beats
  "This is for high-achievers who feel invisible." Make them feel seen, not named.
- **No spoiler, no overpromise.** Leave the loop open. Promise only what the video
  actually delivers — if it overpromises, it's clickbait and watch time dies.
- **Plain language.** No jargon, no fluff, no hashtag soup. A few clean lines.
- **Repel the wrong viewer.** The framing should let the anti-audience feel "this
  isn't for me" — that's a feature, it protects watch time.

## Length

Target ~600 characters total. Never exceed 5000 (YouTube's cap). Shorter and
sharper beats longer.
