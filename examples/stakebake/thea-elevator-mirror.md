# Stakebake example — Thea ("She Asked Too Much. I Said No This Time.")

> **EXAMPLE ONLY — not a rule, not a template.** One concrete illustration of a
> strong stakebake, kept for reference/understanding. The rules live in
> `stakebake_generator.md` and `stakebake_evaluator.md`. The engine does NOT load
> this during generation, and its specifics (Thea, the elevator mirror, etc.)
> must never be copied into another story.

🔥 Stakes Identified:
– Emotional: Thea's sense of self is on the line. If she doesn't reclaim moments like this, she continues to internalize the belief that she's invisible, unworthy, and powerless.
– Spiritual: This is her first real act of spiritual authorship — proof that her inner world can create change.
– Identity-based: Saying "no" for the first time isn't just a choice — it's a confrontation with her old self-concept.
– Hope vs. despair: This isn't just a story about one scene — it's whether Thea can start believing that change is possible.
– Relatability: Every viewer has a moment like this: the swallowed "no," the smile that wasn't real, the tiredness that went unnoticed. You're handing them a tool instead of a wallow.

💥 Ways to Raise the Stakes:
– Name the cost of not revising: A single moment unprocessed becomes a brick in the wall of her false identity. "One moment" seems small — but it builds the entire house.
– Tighten the transformation window: Emphasize that she's not revising to "heal her past," but to stop this moment from calcifying. That urgency makes it real.
– Show the old pattern failing in real-time: Instead of just telling us she's tried affirmations and LOA content before, dramatize what it felt like when those things didn't help. Give us the sting of their failure.
– Punch up the win: The win isn't just relief — it's agency. She doesn't feel better because she vented. She feels better because she authored a new version and embodied it.
– Invite the viewer to do it too: Even a soft call to action — a "pause and try this with me" moment — could make this more than just a story. It becomes a tool in their hand.
