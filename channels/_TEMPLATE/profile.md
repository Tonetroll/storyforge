## FULL POSITIONING  (the deep stages — idea, theme, story — also receive everything below)

# TARGET AUDIENCE POSITIONING TEMPLATE

## 1. Video / Offer / Channel Topic

**What is this video or offer about?**
Write the clear topic, transformation, or emotional problem.

Example:
This video is about using revision to stop reliving emotional rejection and reclaim inner power.

---

## 2. Audience Avatar

**Name:**
Give the avatar a name.

**Age:**
How old are they?

**Gender / Identity:**
Who are they primarily?

**Location:**
Where do they live or culturally belong?
Examples: U.S., Canada, Great Britain, Europe, urban areas, spiritually curious communities.

**Education Level:**
Are they highly educated, self-taught, academic, creative, professional, etc.?

**Income / Life Stability:**
What does their outer life look like financially and practically?

**Relationship / Family Status:**
Are they single, partnered, divorced, child-free, a parent, emotionally unavailable, etc.?

**Cultural / Family Background:**
What shaped their emotional patterns?
Examples: achievement culture, emotional repression, religious background, immigrant family, high-performance family, chaotic upbringing, emotionally distant parents.

---

## 3. Outer Life

**What does their life look like on paper?**
Describe their job, achievements, routines, social image, responsibilities, and what others assume about them.

**What are they praised for?**
Examples: discipline, intelligence, kindness, success, being helpful, being strong, staying calm, being reliable.

**What do people misunderstand about them?**
What do others not see beneath the surface?

---

## 4. Inner World

**What do they secretly feel?**
Examples: unseen, exhausted, disconnected, resentful, numb, lonely, spiritually hungry, tired of performing.

**What are they starting to realize?**
What truth is beginning to break through?

**What are they afraid will happen if nothing changes?**
This is the deeper emotional fear.

Example:
She fears she will keep achieving, giving, fixing, and proving herself — and still never feel deeply loved, chosen, or known.

---

## 5. Core Emotional Wound

**What pain is driving their search?**
Choose one primary wound.

Examples:
“I am only loved when I perform.”
“I have to be useful to be wanted.”
“If I stop achieving, I disappear.”
“My feelings are too much.”
“I can be admired, but not truly known.”
“I always have to understand others, but no one understands me.”

---

## 6. Current Beliefs

**What do they currently believe about themselves?**

**What do they currently believe about love, success, money, health, or spirituality?**

**What belief has kept them stuck?**

Example:
She believes that if she becomes impressive enough, disciplined enough, healed enough, or spiritual enough, she will finally feel safe and wanted.

---

## 7. Desired Transformation

**What do they consciously want?**
This is what they would say they want.

Examples:
Confidence, love, peace, success, clarity, better relationships, emotional healing.

**What do they actually want underneath that?**
This is the deeper soul-level desire.

Examples:
To feel whole.
To stop abandoning herself.
To feel emotionally safe.
To be loved without performing.
To trust her inner reality.
To feel connected to God, self, life, and desire again.

---

## 8. Goals

**Practical goals:**
What do they want to change externally?

**Emotional goals:**
How do they want to feel?

**Spiritual goals:**
What deeper truth or alignment are they seeking?

**Identity goals:**
Who are they becoming?

Example:
She wants to stop chasing proof outside herself and become someone who lives from inner knowing, emotional honesty, and spiritual authority.

---

## 9. Psychographics

**Personality:**
How do they think, react, relate, and cope?

**Values:**
What matters most to them?

Examples:
Truth, emotional depth, freedom, integrity, spiritual growth, love, self-respect, transformation.

**Hobbies / Interests:**
What do they consume or do?

Examples:
Personal growth, manifestation, fitness, journaling, psychology, spirituality, podcasts, books, movement, nature, art, music.

**Content habits:**
What kind of content do they watch now?

**What are they tired of seeing?**
Examples: motivational fluff, toxic positivity, recycled manifestation advice, shallow “high vibe” content, fake spiritual perfection.

---

## 10. Challenges & Pain Points

**External problem:**
What is visibly not working?

**Internal problem:**
What emotional pattern is underneath?

**Spiritual problem:**
Where are they disconnected from truth, imagination, faith, or inner authority?

**Relational problem:**
How does this show up with other people?

**Self-abandonment pattern:**
Where do they betray themselves to stay safe, loved, approved of, or successful?

---

## 11. Trigger Moment

**What moment makes them click the video?**
Describe the emotional situation happening right before they watch.

Examples:
She just had a conversation where she felt invisible again.
She achieved something and felt nothing.
She watched someone choose less effort and still receive love.
She realized her affirmations are not touching the real wound.
She felt calm on the outside but abandoned on the inside.

---

## 12. What They Need to Hear

**What truth would stop them mid-scroll?**

**What sentence would feel like relief?**

**What sentence would feel confronting but accurate?**

Example:
You are not tired because you are failing. You are tired because you built a life around being impressive instead of being honest.

---

## 13. Positioning Statement

**This video is for:**
Describe exactly who it is for.

**It helps them:**
Name the transformation.

**By showing them:**
Name the method, insight, or emotional shift.

Template:
This video is for [specific person] who feels [specific emotional pain] even though [outer contradiction]. It helps them [desired transformation] by showing them [new paradigm, method, or realization].

Example:
This video is for the high-achieving woman who looks successful but feels emotionally disconnected. It helps her understand why external success has not created inner safety — and shows her how imagination, revision, and emotional truth can become the beginning of real transformation.

---

## 14. Why This Video Matters Now

**Why does this person need this now?**

**What has changed in their awareness?**

**Why are they finally ready?**

Example:
She is no longer satisfied with understanding spiritual ideas intellectually. She wants to feel them, apply them, and live differently.

---

## 15. Purchasing / Commitment Process

**What makes them trust you?**

**What makes them hesitate?**

**What would make them invest time, money, or attention?**

**What emotional proof do they need before they commit?**

Example:
She invests when she feels the content is emotionally honest, grounded, and not trying to manipulate her with false promises. She needs to feel that the offer sees the real wound, not just the surface desire.

# ANTI-AUDIENCE TEMPLATE

## 16. Who This Is Not For

**Anti-avatar name:**
Give them a clear label.

Example: Spiritual Sarah.

**Summary:**
Who is this person, and why are they not aligned with the content?

**What do they believe?**

**What do they want instead of real transformation?**

**What kind of content do they prefer?**

**Why would they reject this video?**

Example:
This is not for the person who wants spirituality to keep them comfortable. It is not for someone who uses affirmations to avoid grief, anger, boundaries, or honest self-confrontation.

---

## 17. Anti-Audience Goals

**What are they trying to get?**

Examples:
To feel good without changing.
To stay positive without facing pain.
To manifest without emotional honesty.
To be spiritually validated without taking responsibility.

---

## 18. Anti-Audience Beliefs

**What beliefs keep them away from this content?**

Examples:
“If I feel bad, I am doing something wrong.”
“Anger is low vibration.”
“Good people don’t have boundaries.”
“If I affirm enough, I don’t have to feel the wound.”
“Real spirituality should always feel peaceful.”

---

## 19. Anti-Audience Pain Points

**What do they avoid feeling?**

**What truths can they not tolerate yet?**

**What responsibility are they not ready to take?**

---

## 20. Why This Content Rejects Them

**What does your video refuse to reward?**

Examples:
Emotional bypassing.
Performative healing.
Spiritual superiority.
Avoiding accountability.
Using positivity as a shield.
Wanting the result without embodiment.

Template:
This content is not designed to comfort [anti-avatar] in their avoidance. It challenges [specific pattern] and rewards [specific value], which means it will naturally repel people who want [surface desire] without [real transformation].

# FINAL VIDEO POSITIONING CHECK

Before creating the video, answer these:

1. Who exactly is this video for?

2. What emotional state are they in before watching?

3. What outer problem do they think they have?

4. What inner problem do they actually have?

5. What truth are they ready to hear?

6. What truth are they not ready to hear — but need?

7. What transformation does the video promise?

8. What does the video refuse to validate?

9. Who will feel deeply seen by this?

10. Who will feel exposed or annoyed by this?

11. What belief should the viewer question by the end?

12. What new identity should feel possible by the end?

13. Why is this different from generic self-help or spiritual content?

14. What would make this feel emotionally real instead of performative?

15. What line, image, or moment proves that this video is for [your avatar] and not for [your anti-avatar]?

# ONE-SENTENCE POSITIONING FORMULA

This video is for [specific avatar] who is [emotional state] because [core wound or conflict], and it helps her [desired shift] by showing her that [new belief or spiritual truth] — while refusing to validate [anti-audience pattern].

Example:

This video is for the high-achieving woman who feels emotionally invisible despite doing everything “right,” and it helps her begin reclaiming herself by showing that imagination is not escape — it is the place where identity is rebuilt — while refusing to validate spiritual bypassing, fake positivity, or self-abandonment dressed as peace.
